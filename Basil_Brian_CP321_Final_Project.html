Section 2. Data Set Description, Statistics, and Processing¶

The dataset is in a CSV (Comma Seperated Values) format. We had to go in and delete a bunch of "information" data which told us stuff like what does "Z" or "X" mean under the "Fuel Type". We didn't really care for this information so went and cleaned it up a little.

The rows we have are: Model year, which shows us when the car is being manufactured. The Make, which shows us the company that makes the car. The Vehicle Class, this shows us the classification type of the vehicle (SUV, Compact, Minivan, etc...) The Cylinders, Transmission, Fuel Consumption and Emissions didn't tell us a lot about the data so we ommitted using it.

The rating for both CO2 and Smog is what we decided to focus on, since we can easily deduce how the government ranks the vechicle class per make on their CO2 Emissions and Smog Emissions. The rating is out of 10, where 1 is Worse and 10 is Best for the environment.

The statistics were taken from Natural Resources Canada's website from the Government of Canada and compiled into a series of CSV formatted files. Not much manual processing was required, but we had to fix the headers since they were seperated into two rows (some did not have any value which would've made processing thru Pandas a lot harder). You are able to see our original dataframe in the cell below.

Section 3. Visualization¶

The first visualization we see is for CO2 ratings for each vehicle class in the year 2020. We decided to make all the graphs into horizontal bar charts and sorted from greatest to least value, since it help us visualize the comparsions between them all. We can clearly see in the first one (since 1=worse and 10=best) that Passenger Vans are rated worse for CO2 emissions on average, while Small Station wagons are ideally sitting around 6.3 for their CO2 rating.

Conclusion¶

In conclusion looking at the visualisations we can see that passenger vans are really bad for the environment since their ratings are 1 and 2.5 for CO2 and smog respectively. We can otherwise see that small station wagons have a very high CO2 rating and the 2nd highest smog rating for the vehicle classes, the average being 6.5 and 5.5 respectively. This is interesting since we would've assumed that bigger cars are worse for the environment.

For the manufacturers we can see that Bugatti on average, has the lowest ratings for CO2 and smog with a rating of 1. Honda having the best rating for CO2 while MINI has the best rating for smog. This may be due to the fact that MINI have smaller cars and therefore overall a smaller carbon footprint. Hondas are usually made as fuel-efficient cars and that may be the reason they're so high rated for their CO2 emissions. Bugatti cars are made with the "newest" and "fastest" technology in mind, and doing some further research we can clearly see that Bugatti cars are voted as the worst environment offenders by the EPA. They're expensive killers.

From doing this assignment we can only assume that any cars created by companies focusing on "powerful" and "sporty" are, on average, worse for the environment than a Station Wagon or Minivan from a company like Honda, which also may have Eco settings to help reduce their carbon footprints.
